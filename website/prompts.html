Everyday Prompt Templates

Step 5 of 7

These are real, ready-to-use prompts. Copy them and fill in the parts in [square brackets] with your own details.

Writing & Communication

Write an email

Use when you need to write something but aren't sure how to phrase it.

"Write a [short / formal / friendly] email to [who] about [topic]. The key point I want to make is [your point]. Keep it [under X sentences / polite / direct]."
Improve something you've written

Paste in your text and ask Claude to make it better.

"Here's something I wrote: [paste your text]. Can you improve the tone and clarity, without changing the meaning? Keep it roughly the same length."
Write a bio or introduction
"Write a short professional bio for me. I am [your name], I work as [your job], and I [one interesting thing about you]. Keep it to 3 sentences and make it sound confident but not boastful."

Learning & Research

Explain something simply
"Explain [topic] to me like I've never heard of it before. Use a simple real-life example and keep it short."
Summarise a long article or document
"Here is an article / document: [paste text]. Please summarise the key points in plain English. Use bullet points and keep it brief."
Quiz me on a topic
"I want to learn about [topic]. Can you quiz me with 5 questions to test what I already know? Start easy and get harder. Tell me if I'm right or wrong after each answer."

Planning & Thinking

Brainstorm ideas
"I need ideas for [project / gift / trip / situation]. Give me 10 different options, ranging from simple to creative. I'm looking for [any specific constraints, e.g. budget, time, audience]."
Help make a decision
"I'm trying to decide between [option A] and [option B]. Here's my situation: [describe briefly]. What are the pros and cons of each? What would you suggest and why?"
Create a simple plan
"Help me make a step-by-step plan for [goal]. I have [X weeks / days / budget]. Break it down into clear actions I can take, one at a time."

Everyday Life

Understand something confusing
"I received this [letter / email / document]: [paste text]. Can you explain what it means in plain English and tell me if there's anything I need to do?"
Prepare for a conversation or meeting
"I have a [meeting / interview / difficult conversation] with [who] about [topic]. Can you help me prepare? What are the key things I should say or ask? What should I be ready for?"
