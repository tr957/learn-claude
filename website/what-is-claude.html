What is Claude?

Step 1 of 7

Claude is an AI assistant made by a company called Anthropic. You type a message to it — just like texting a friend — and it writes back. It understands plain English, so you never need to use special commands or code.

How is it different from Google?

Google finds web pages and shows you a list of links. Claude actually reads, understands, and responds to you directly. You can have a back-and-forth conversation, ask follow-up questions, and say things like "explain that more simply" — and it will.

Think of it this way: Google is a library catalogue. Claude is a very knowledgeable friend sitting across the table from you, happy to discuss anything.

What can you use it for?

Here are some everyday things real people use Claude for:

Writing

  • Drafting emails you're not sure how to phrase
  • Improving something you've already written
  • Writing cover letters, bios, or introductions
  • Coming up with captions, social posts, or messages

Learning

  • Getting a plain-English explanation of anything confusing
  • Asking "what does this word / term / concept mean?"
  • Studying for an exam by asking Claude to quiz you
  • Understanding a document, contract, or article

Planning & thinking

  • Brainstorming ideas for a project, gift, or trip
  • Thinking through a difficult decision
  • Making a to-do list or schedule
  • Getting feedback on a plan before you commit to it

Summarising

  • Pasting in a long article and asking for the key points
  • Summarising meeting notes
  • Getting the gist of a long email thread

Is it safe to use?

Claude is designed to be helpful, honest, and harmless. It won't help with harmful requests, and it will tell you when it's unsure about something rather than making things up. That said, always double-check important facts — no AI is perfect.

Do I need to pay?

Claude has a free tier at claude.ai that is more than enough for most people. A paid plan exists for heavier use, but it is not required to get started.

Best first things to try

Once you're signed up, try one of these to get a feel for it:

  • "Explain what you are in simple terms"
  • "I have a job interview on Friday. What are 5 things I should prepare?"
  • "Explain [something confusing to you] like I'm a complete beginner"
  • "Help me write a short email to [describe the situation]"
