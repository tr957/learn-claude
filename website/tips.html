Tips & Examples

The way you phrase your message to Claude makes a big difference. Here are some simple tips with real before-and-after examples.

Tip 1: Be specific

The more detail you give, the more useful the answer.

Vague

"Write something about dogs."

Specific

"Write a 3-sentence description of golden retrievers for a children's book."

Tip 2: Give Claude a role

Tell Claude who to act as and it will adjust its tone and style.

Without a role

"Explain photosynthesis."

With a role

"Explain photosynthesis as if you're a teacher talking to a 10-year-old."

Tip 3: Ask for a specific format

Claude can structure its answer however you need.

No format

"What are the benefits of exercise?"

With format

"List 5 benefits of exercise as short bullet points."

Tip 4: Say "try again" freely

If an answer isn't quite right, just say so. Claude won't be offended. Try: "That's too long — can you shorten it?" or "Can you make it more casual?"
