How to Ask Better Questions

Step 3 of 7

Claude is only as good as the message you send it. A vague question gets a vague answer. A clear, specific message gets something genuinely useful. This page shows you how.

The three things that matter most

1. Context — Tell Claude who you are, what you're trying to do, or why you need it.
2. Specifics — The more detail you give, the better the answer. Vague in = vague out.
3. Format — Tell Claude how you want the answer. A list? A short paragraph? Step by step?

Bad, Better, Best — Writing

Scenario: You need to write an email to your boss asking for a day off.

Bad

"Write an email about time off."

Better

"Write an email to my boss asking for a day off next Friday."

Best

"Write a short, professional email to my manager asking for next Friday off. I want to attend a family event. Keep it polite and under 5 sentences."

Bad, Better, Best — Learning something new

Scenario: You want to understand what inflation is.

Bad

"Explain inflation."

Better

"Explain inflation in simple terms."

Best

"Explain inflation like I'm someone who has never studied economics. Use a simple real-life example and keep it to a short paragraph."

Bad, Better, Best — Getting feedback

Scenario: You've written something and want to improve it.

Bad

"Is this good?" [pastes text]

Better

"Can you improve this email I wrote?" [pastes text]

Best

"I wrote this email to a new client. Can you make it sound more confident and professional, without changing the meaning? Keep it roughly the same length." [pastes text]

Give Claude a role

Telling Claude who to be changes the tone and style of its answer dramatically. This is one of the simplest tricks that makes the biggest difference.

Without a role

"Explain this legal document to me."

With a role

"Act as a plain-English legal translator. Explain what this document means in simple terms that a non-lawyer would understand." [pastes document]

Quick reference

  • Be specific — include who, what, why, and how long/short
  • Add context — Claude can't read your mind. Tell it the situation.
  • Give it a role — "Act as a…" changes everything
  • Ask for a format — "as a bullet list", "in one paragraph", "step by step"
  • Say who it's for — "explain this to a 10-year-old" vs "explain this to a professional"
