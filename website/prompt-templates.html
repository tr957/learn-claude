Prompt Templates

Every copyable prompt from this site, organised by workflow. Fill in the [square brackets].

Everyday

Summarise a document
"Here is a [whitepaper / report / article] on [topic]: [paste text]. Summarise the key points in [number] bullet points. Focus on: [what matters — e.g. technology, team, market, risks]."
Explain a concept
"Explain [concept / protocol / term] in plain English. I understand the basics of crypto but not the technical details. Cover: what problem it solves, how it works at a high level, and who the main players are."
Structure rough notes
"Here are my rough notes: [paste]. Organise these into: key takeaways, open questions, and suggested next steps."
Compare options
"Compare [Option A] and [Option B] across these dimensions: [list]. Present as a table. Note where information is incomplete."
Stress-test your thinking
"Here's my current thinking on [topic]: [paste]. What are the strongest counterarguments? What am I potentially missing?"

Research & Diligence

First-pass on a project
"Here is a description of a project: [paste]. Give me a first-pass view covering: what it does, the problem it's solving, who the target users are, and any obvious risks or red flags."
Draft a project brief
"Here are my research notes on [project]: [paste]. Draft a first-pass project brief with sections: Overview, Problem & Solution, Market & Differentiation, Team, Key Risks, Open Questions."
Compare projects
"Compare [Project A] and [Project B] across: technology approach, target market, tokenomics, team background, and traction. Present as a table. Flag where information is limited."
Summarise research for a meeting
"I've gathered the following research on [project]: [paste]. Summarise into a 5-minute briefing: What they do, Why it's interesting, Key concerns, Questions to resolve."
Due diligence checklist
"I'm preparing due diligence on [project]. Here's what I know: [paste]. Generate a due diligence checklist covering: technical architecture, team, market & competition, legal & regulatory risks, tokenomics, and ecosystem."

Founder Meetings

Get up to speed before a call
"Here is the website / whitepaper for [project]: [paste]. Give me: a one-paragraph overview, 3 things that seem interesting or differentiated, and 3 things I should probe on in the meeting."
Generate sharp questions
"I'm meeting with the founders of [project], which is [one-line description]. Generate 10 specific questions covering: product differentiation, go-to-market, tokenomics, team background, and key risks."
Turn call notes into an internal summary
"Here are my notes from a founder call with [project]: [paste]. Turn these into a structured internal summary with: Overview, Key Points Discussed, What's Compelling, Concerns & Open Questions, Recommended Next Steps."
Extract action items
"Here are my notes from a call with [project]: [paste]. Extract all action items. Present as two lists: things we said we'd do, and things they said they'd do."
Draft a follow-up message
"Draft a short follow-up to a founder after a first meeting. Things to reference: [list]. Include: appreciation, 1–2 things we found interesting, and the agreed next step. Keep it brief and professional."

Writing & Internal Comms

Turn notes into a memo
"Here are my rough notes: [paste]. Turn these into a clean internal memo. Structure: one-paragraph summary, key points, recommended actions. Under 300 words."
Structure a presentation
"I'm preparing a presentation on [topic] for [audience]. Key points: [paste]. Structure into a 5-slide outline with: slide title, one-sentence summary, and 3 bullet points per slide."
Draft outreach
"Draft a short, professional outreach message to [person / team] at [project]. Purpose: [why you're reaching out]. Tone: direct but warm. Under 5 sentences. We are 1kx."
Improve a draft
"Here's something I wrote: [paste]. Improve the clarity and conciseness. Keep the same meaning and don't change the tone significantly."

Portfolio Support

Understand a portfolio request
"Here is a request from one of our portfolio companies: [paste]. Summarise what they're asking for, what the underlying problem is, and what a good response would include."
Write a briefing note
"I need to brief a colleague on [portfolio company]. Here's what I know: [paste]. Write a short briefing note covering: what they do, where they are, current priorities, and open items from our side."
Draft a response
"Draft a response to this request from [portfolio company]: [paste request]. My answer is: [paste notes]. Make it clear, helpful, and professional. Keep it concise."
