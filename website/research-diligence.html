Research & Diligence

Scouting, briefs, comparisons, and diligence prep — faster.

Scouting a new project

When to use: You've come across a project and want a fast first-pass view before deciding whether to look deeper.

What to paste in: The project's website copy, whitepaper intro, or any description you have.

"Here is a brief description of a project: [paste]. Give me a first-pass view covering: what it does, the problem it's solving, who the target users are, and any obvious risks or red flags."
"I'm doing a first look at [project name]. Here is their whitepaper introduction: [paste]. What are the 3 most important questions I should investigate before going further?"

Writing a first-pass project brief

When to use: You've done initial research and want to turn your notes into a structured brief to share internally.

What to paste in: Your research notes, any relevant documents, and the structure you want to use.

"Here are my research notes on [project]: [paste notes]. Draft a first-pass project brief with the following sections: Overview, Problem & Solution, Market & Differentiation, Team, Key Risks, Open Questions."
"Based on the following information about [project], write a concise project brief (max 400 words) for internal review. Highlight what's interesting and what needs more investigation. Information: [paste]."

Comparing projects or competitors

When to use: You're evaluating multiple projects in the same space and want a structured side-by-side view.

What to paste in: Notes or descriptions for each project. The more detail per project, the better the comparison.

"Compare [Project A] and [Project B] across the following dimensions: technology approach, target market, tokenomics, team background, and current traction. Present as a structured table. Flag where information is limited or unclear."
"Here are notes on three projects in the [sector] space: [paste]. Which looks most differentiated and why? What are the key trade-offs between them from an investment perspective?"
"I'm trying to understand the competitive landscape for [space]. Here are the main players I know of: [list]. What are the key dimensions to compare them on, and based on what I've shared, how do they stack up?"

Summarising research for a partner meeting

When to use: You've done research on a project and need to condense it into something sharp before a team or partner discussion.

"I've gathered the following research on [project]: [paste]. Summarise this into a 5-minute briefing document. Structure it as: What they do, Why it's interesting, Key concerns, and Questions to resolve."
"Here are my notes on [project]: [paste]. Write a 3-paragraph summary I can share before a partner meeting. Be direct about what's compelling and what's uncertain."

Due diligence prep

When to use: You're moving into deeper diligence and want to make sure you're asking the right questions across the right areas.

"I'm preparing due diligence on [project]. Here's what I know so far: [paste]. Generate a due diligence checklist covering: technical architecture, team & background, market & competition, legal & regulatory risks, tokenomics, and community/ecosystem."
"Based on this project description, what are the 5 most important things to verify independently during diligence? [paste description]"
"Here is a technical whitepaper: [paste]. Summarise the key technical claims being made and flag any that seem unsubstantiated or that I should verify with an engineer."
Important: Claude doesn't have real-time internet access and may have outdated information about specific projects. Use it to structure thinking and draft documents — always verify facts and figures from primary sources.
