Using Claude Safely at Work

How to use AI responsibly in a professional setting.

Claude is a powerful tool for accelerating work, but using it well in a professional context means being thoughtful about what you share and how you use its outputs. These principles apply to everyone at 1kx.

Think before you paste

Not everything should be pasted into Claude without thought. Before sharing information, ask yourself: would I be comfortable if this appeared outside this conversation?

Be careful with

Confidential deal information at a sensitive stage, personal data about founders or individuals, internal discussions that aren't yours to share, anything under NDA, proprietary investment theses, or LP-related information.

A practical rule: Describe the situation in general terms rather than pasting in specifics. You can often get 90% of the value from Claude by saying "a founder in the DeFi space" instead of naming them, or "a project we're diligencing" instead of naming it.

Check outputs before sharing

Claude produces fluent, professional-sounding text — but that doesn't mean it's always accurate. Before sharing anything Claude wrote with someone outside your immediate team, read it carefully and verify any facts, figures, or claims it makes.

  • Don't forward a Claude-written summary without reading it first
  • Don't send a Claude-drafted message without checking the tone and content are right
  • For anything that will be seen externally, treat Claude's output as a first draft, not a final one

Claude accelerates your thinking — it doesn't replace it

Claude is a thinking tool, not a decision-making tool. It can help you structure your analysis, draft a memo, or generate questions — but the judgment, the relationships, and the decisions remain yours.

Use Claude to move faster, think more clearly, and write better. Don't use it as a substitute for understanding something yourself or for exercising your own judgment.

Be careful with factual claims

Claude can be wrong — especially about specific facts, current events, project details, and anything that has changed recently. Its training data has a cutoff, and it doesn't browse the internet in standard usage.

  • Don't rely on Claude for up-to-date information about a specific project's funding, team, or traction — verify from primary sources
  • Don't cite Claude as a source
  • Treat its outputs on specific factual questions as a starting point for verification, not a final answer

Conversations are not stored on your device

Claude conversations happen on Anthropic's servers. While Anthropic has privacy commitments, the safest assumption is to treat anything you paste into Claude as something that could theoretically be reviewed. Use discretion accordingly.

Summary: four principles

1. Describe, don't expose. Use general terms when working with sensitive information.
2. Read before you share. Claude's outputs are drafts. Review them.
3. Judgment stays with you. Claude accelerates your thinking; it doesn't replace it.
4. Verify facts. Especially for current events, specific projects, and anything you'll share externally.
