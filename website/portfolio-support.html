Portfolio Support

Portfolio Support

Using Claude to help portfolio companies and structure your support.

Understanding a portfolio company request

When to use: A portfolio company has come to you with a request and you want to understand what they actually need before responding.

What to paste in: The message or request as they sent it.

"Here is a request from one of our portfolio companies: [paste request]. Summarise what they're actually asking for, what the underlying problem seems to be, and what a good response would need to include."
"A portfolio company sent this message: [paste]. Is this a clear ask? What information would I need to give them a useful response?"

Preparing a briefing note on a portfolio company

When to use: You need to brief a colleague or partner on a portfolio company — for a call, a review, or an introduction.

"I need to brief a colleague on [portfolio company]. Here's what I know about them: [paste notes]. Write a short briefing note covering: what they do, where they are in their journey, current priorities, and any open items from our side."
"Here are my notes on [portfolio company]: [paste]. Write a 2-paragraph summary a new colleague could read before getting on a call with them."

Drafting a response to a portfolio company

When to use: You have an answer or recommendation in mind and want to communicate it clearly and helpfully.

"Draft a response to this request from [portfolio company]: [paste request]. My answer / view is: [paste your notes or thinking]. Make the response clear, helpful, and professional. Keep it concise."
"A portfolio company asked: [paste question]. Here's what I know: [paste context]. Draft a response that's direct and useful without overcomplicating it."

Structuring a support recommendation

When to use: A portfolio company has asked for help with something significant and you want to give them a structured response or action plan.

"A portfolio company has asked for help with [topic]. Here's what I know about their situation: [paste context]. Draft a structured set of recommendations or a short action plan they could follow. Keep it practical."
"Here's what [portfolio company] is trying to achieve: [paste]. What are the 3–5 most important things they should focus on? Draft brief notes on each."

Summarising portfolio updates

When to use: You've received a portfolio update, investor report, or status message and want the key points quickly.

"Here is an update from [portfolio company]: [paste]. Summarise the key points in 3–5 bullet points. Flag anything that seems to need attention or follow-up from our side."
"Here are updates from multiple portfolio companies: [paste]. Give me a one-line status per company and flag any that need immediate attention."
