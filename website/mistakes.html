Common Beginner Mistakes

Step 4 of 7

These are the things that trip up almost everyone when they first start using Claude. Knowing about them in advance will save you a lot of frustration.

Mistake 1: Being too vague

"Help me with my CV" tells Claude almost nothing. Who are you? What kind of job? What do you want changed? The more context you give, the better the result.

Fix: Pretend you're explaining the situation to a helpful stranger who knows nothing about you.

Mistake 2: Asking everything at once

Packing five questions into one message usually leads to a long, unfocused answer. Claude tries to answer everything but ends up doing none of it well.

Fix: Ask one thing at a time. Have a conversation. Build on each answer.

Mistake 3: Giving up after one bad answer

If the first response isn't what you wanted, it doesn't mean Claude can't help. It often just means your question needs adjusting. Most people stop too early.

Fix: Say what was wrong and ask again. "That was too long — can you shorten it?" or "That's not quite right — I meant X."

Mistake 4: Treating it like a search engine

Typing short keywords like "best restaurants London" won't get good results. Claude is a conversation, not a search bar.

Fix: Write full sentences. Describe what you want and why.

Mistake 5: Not saying who the answer is for

"Explain climate change" can mean a university-level essay or a one-sentence answer for a child. Claude doesn't know which you want unless you say.

Fix: Add "explain this to someone who…" or "for a [beginner / professional / 10-year-old]".

Mistake 6: Mixing too many topics in one conversation

Claude keeps track of your conversation, which is helpful — but if you jump from recipes to legal advice to planning a holiday, it gets confused about what context applies.

Fix: Start a new conversation for each new topic.

Mistake 7: Taking every answer as gospel

Claude is very capable but it can be wrong, especially about facts, dates, and current events. It's a thinking tool, not an encyclopedia.

Fix: For anything important, verify with a reliable source. Use Claude to help you think, then check the facts.

Mistake 8: Not telling Claude the format you want

By default, Claude writes in paragraphs. But sometimes you need a bullet list, a table, a numbered guide, or just two sentences. If you don't say, it guesses.

Fix: End your message with the format you want. "Give me this as a bullet list." "Keep it under 3 sentences."
