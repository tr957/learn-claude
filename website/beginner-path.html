Getting Started

Beginner Path

Start here if you've never used Claude before.

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic. You type a message to it in plain English and it writes back — like a very capable colleague who can help with research, writing, analysis, and thinking. You don't need any technical background to use it well.

How to access Claude

  1. Go to claude.ai in your browser
  2. Sign up with your work email — the free tier is enough to start
  3. Click the text box, type your message, and press Enter
Tip: Ask your team if 1kx has a shared plan — you may already have access to a more powerful version.

What makes a good prompt

Three things make the difference between a vague answer and a useful one:

1. Context — Tell Claude what you're working on and why. It can't read your mind.
2. Specifics — The more detail you give, the better the output. Vague in = vague out.
3. Format — Tell Claude how you want the answer: a bullet list, a short paragraph, a table, a draft email.

Your first 5 prompts to try

These are practical starting points relevant to 1kx work. Copy one and try it now.

Summarise a document
"Here is a whitepaper / article / document: [paste text]. Summarise the key points in 5 bullet points for an investment audience."
Explain a concept quickly
"Explain [concept, protocol, or term] in plain English. I understand the basics of crypto but not the technical details."
Draft a message
"Draft a short, professional message to [recipient] about [topic]. Keep it direct and under 5 sentences."
Structure rough notes
"Here are my rough notes from a call: [paste notes]. Organise these into: key takeaways, open questions, and next steps."
Prepare for a founder meeting
"I'm about to meet with a founder building [describe project]. Generate 8 sharp questions I should ask about their differentiation, go-to-market, and team."

The most common beginner mistake

Being too vague. "Help me with this project" tells Claude almost nothing. Instead, describe the situation: what you're working on, what you need, and how you want it formatted. The more context you give, the better the result.

Too vague

"Summarise this for me." [pastes document]

Much better

"Here is a whitepaper on [topic]: [paste]. Summarise the key points in 5 bullet points. Focus on: what it does, who it's for, and what makes it different from existing solutions."

Refining answers

If the first answer isn't quite right, just say what's wrong: "That's too long — can you shorten it?" or "Focus more on the technical risks." Claude remembers the conversation and will adjust.
